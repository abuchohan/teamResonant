Supermakets

As a student, you will mostly be searching for cost friendly supermarkets around the campus area, so here are some of the most visited:

  • ALDI (location)
    • A well-known chain around the UK, ALDI is the place where you will get the most for your money. Unfortunately, it is located in the city center which is about a 20-30 minute walk from the campus and you might not want to carry all of your shopping from there.
  • ASDA (location)
    • At ASDA you can find anything for your fridge, whether you want fresh or frozen, fast food or the right spice for a specific dish you can find it there.
  • Sainsbury's (location/s)
    • Sainsbury's has the most shops out of all of them however the variety of products is not that big and the prices are a bit higher because the locations are conveniently placed. They are ideal for small shopping.
  • Local shops
    • Local shops don't tend to be price friendly. They also do not accept card payments from some international banks and prefer cash. However, they are the closest to the accommodations out of all of them.

Shopping - Tips and Tricks

If you do not know what to cook and what to buy, take a recipe for a dish you like from the web and buy the ingredients. Or get inspired by our food ideas.
If you want to save money and you have a friend you can trust you can buy larger amounts of food and split it.
If you don’t want to deal with crowds of people make sure to avoid shopping on Friday and Saturday.
Sessions are the least likely time you will want to go shopping so make sure you have enough food to put your studies on priority.
Tips on revision

    • Curry and Rice

    (Time: 15min) (premade sauce)
    Simple and consistent dish every time. Quick and easy.
    Ingredients:
    -400g Chicken
    -2 Cups rice
    2 tbsp. Oil
    2 tbsp. salt and pepper
    Optional:
    Cinnamon stick

    Step 1.
    Pre heat oven to 200 degrees (Fan oven) Leave for 20 minutes to pre heat oven, this way your chicken will cook evenly. Wash, clean and cut the chicken in strips or cubes. Put on a baking tray and a drizzle of oil and leave for 14 minutes.
    Step 2.
    Clean rice until the water is clear. Drain the water and add 4 cups of water (2 cups for 1 cup of rice. Put in oil salt and pepper. (Add cinnamon to rice to give it fragrance). Bring to boil and then turn heat to low and leave until water is gone.
    Step 3.
    After chicken is done heat up frying pan and pour the curry sauce in there. Add chicken then reduce heat and simmer for 5-10 minutes.
    Step 4.
    Pour the rice into plate and add the desired amount of chicken with sauce. Then dig in! You can enjoy this with garlic bread.

    Keema

    Prep time: 5 Mins
    Cook time: 25 Minutes
    (Serves 4, but does it really?)
    Really easy to make Indian dish. You can make it with one pot a knife and a cutting board. Goes really well with pitta bread.
    Ingredients:
    -500g Mince Meat
    -2 fresh tomatoes
    -2 Medium Onions
    -1 Tbsp. Chilli Powder (Can add more to taste)
    -1 Tbsp. Turmeric Powder
    -1 Frozen Cube of Ginger and Garlic
    -1 Tbsp. Salt and Pepper
    -1 Tbsp. Cumin Powder

    Step 1. (Prep)
    Firstly finely dice your onion. Chop up the tomatoes. Get a small bowl and add all the following spices into the bowl.
    -1 Tbsp. Chilli Powder (Can add more to taste)
    -1 Tbsp. Turmeric Powder
    -1 Frozen Cube of Ginger and Garlic
    -1 Tbsp. Salt and Pepper
    -1 Tbsp. Cumin Powder
    Step 2.
    Get a pot with and put a little squirt of oil and turn the heat up. Add the diced onions to the pot and brown.
    Step 3.
    Add the chopped tomatoes once the onions are brown. Add them to the pot along with the meat. And the spices. Mix well. Lower the heat and Leave to cook for 20 minutes.
    Step 4.
    After 20 minutes turn back up to full heat until it isn’t too runny(This might take a you a couple of times after making it to get a consistency you like).
    After this enjoy! Serving suggestion with Pitta bread. that's how i done it quite light tone
